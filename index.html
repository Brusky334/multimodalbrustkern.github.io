The Core Argument

We are standing at an inflection point.

The classical computers we use in our everyday lives, such as phones, laptops, and streaming servers, are reaching their limit. They have been steadily getting faster for a long time, but we are rapidly approaching the physical limit of space on a silicon chip. So that grow will soon slow.

Quantum computers do not try to beat classical machines at their own game. They play an entirely different game. One governed by the strange rules of quantum physics and mechanics. They won't replace your laptop or phone for browsing the web, but for specific types of problems, they will be exponentially more powerful than any other computer we have ever built.

This site explores three questions: What makes them so different? Why are they so hard to build? And what will change when we succeed?
