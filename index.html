The Core Argument

We are standing at an inflection point.

Classical computers, the ones powering your phone, your laptop, the servers streaming your videos, have been getting faster for 60 years by packing more transistors onto silicon. But we are approaching the physical limits of that approach.

Quantum computers do not try to beat classical machines at their own game. They play an entirely different game — one governed by the strange rules of quantum mechanics. They will not replace your laptop for browsing the web, but for specific classes of problems, they will be orders of magnitude more powerful than anything we have ever built.

This site explores three questions: What makes them so different? Why are they so hard to build? And what will change when we succeed?
